001 He who is often reproved, yet stiffens his neck, will suddenly be broken beyond healing. 
002 When the righteous increase, the people rejoice, but when the wicked rule, the people groan. 
003 He who loves wisdom makes his father glad, but a companion of prostitutes squanders his wealth. 
004 By justice a king builds up the land, but he who exacts gifts tears it down. 
005 A man who flatters his neighbor spreads a net for his feet. 
006 An evil man is ensnared in his transgression, but a righteous man sings and rejoices. 
007 A righteous man knows the rights of the poor; a wicked man does not understand such knowledge. 
008 Scoffers set a city aflame, but the wise turn away wrath. 
009 If a wise man has an argument with a fool, the fool only rages and laughs, and there is no quiet. 
010 Bloodthirsty men hate one who is blameless and seek the life of the upright. 
011 A fool gives full vent to his spirit, but a wise man quietly holds it back. 
012 If a ruler listens to falsehood, all his officials will be wicked. 
013 The poor man and the oppressor meet together; the LORD gives light to the eyes of both. 
014 If a king faithfully judges the poor, his throne will be established forever. 
015 The rod and reproof give wisdom, but a child left to himself brings shame to his mother. 
016 When the wicked increase, transgression increases, but the righteous will look upon their downfall. 
017 Discipline your son, and he will give you rest; he will give delight to your heart. 
018 Where there is no prophetic vision the people cast off restraint, but blessed is he who keeps the law. 
019 By mere words a servant is not disciplined, for though he understands, he will not respond. 
020 Do you see a man who is hasty in his words? There is more hope for a fool than for him. 
021 Whoever pampers his servant from childhood will in the end find him his heir. 
022 A man of wrath stirs up strife, and one given to anger causes much transgression. 
023 One's pride will bring him low, but he who is lowly in spirit will obtain honor. 
024 The partner of a thief hates his own life; he hears the curse, but discloses nothing. 
025 The fear of man lays a snare, but whoever trusts in the LORD is safe. 
026 Many seek the face of a ruler, but it is from the LORD that a man gets justice. 
027 An unjust man is an abomination to the righteous, but one whose way is straight is an abomination to the wicked.
